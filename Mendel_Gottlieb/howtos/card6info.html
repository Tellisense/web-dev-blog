Starting Your Project: The Right Way

So, you've decided to begin a new web development project. But you know that you are going to mess something up at the very beginning causing your instructor to loose his mind. That is why you have come here; to ensure that that doesn't happen.

So before you even begin to make any project, it is highly recommended that you create a designated web development folder where you will place all future projects. Once you have done this you are now ready to create a new folder on your desktop. Name the folder as something relevant to your project then drag it into your web-dev-folder. You are now ready to begin your project.

Once you have done this it is now time to link your documents together. In the head tag which was automatically added in with the last step, you are going to type link and then hit tab. This will generate the entire link tag. Inside the quotes following the indication "href" you are going to specify the css document you want linked to your project.

Next step is your UNIVERSAL PRESETS. This step is going to take you over to your styles.css page. The first thing you will do is hit shift 8 which will produce this symbol: * . This selects everything in your HTML document. Next, add these brackets {} which are the primary tools for a css tag. Inside these you are going to type margin the hit tab which will generate the correct colins and semicolins. Inside the symbols you will type 0px. Repeat this step with the tag of padding. Next type in box-sizing and hit tab which will generate the correct symbols. Inside type border-box. This will neutralize any unintentional padding and all elements will be in their correct sizes.

Your final step is to open your terminal by hitting control ` (mac) or ctrl ` (windows). This icon is called a tilda and it's located to the left of the 1 button. For more info on the terminal and what goes in it, click below. You are now ready to begin your web development project.
